Technical Description

PSD2 instructs banks to provide a fully productive XS2A interface till September 2019. In order to guarantee the compliance of this deadline due to adaptions and bugs, PSD2 claims the banks to provide a functional sandbox offering the XS2A services in a non-productive environment. One requirement of the sandbox is the possibility to authenticate Payment Service Providers (PSP) using eIDAS Certificates. Those certificates usually get issued by appropriate Trusted Service Providers (TSP) which are working in cooperation with the National Competent Authoritiy (NCA). Issuing a real certificate just for testing purposes would be a bit too much effort, which is why this application is simulating a fictional TSP issuing Qualified Website Authentication Certificates (QWAC). A QWAC is part of eIDAS and might be better known as X.509 certificate. For PSD2-purposes the certificate gets extended by the QCStatement containing appropriate values such as the role(s) of the PSP. PSD2 consists of Payment Initiation Service (PIS), Account Initiation Service (AIS) and Payment Instrument Issuer Service (PIIS)
